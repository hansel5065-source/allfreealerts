The Best Types of Free Samples You Can Get Right Now

April 8, 2026 · 8 min read · By the AllFreeAlerts Team

Free samples are one of the easiest ways to try new products without spending a dime. But not all free sample categories are created equal. Some industries give away generous, full-sized products regularly. Others offer tiny packets that barely let you test anything. And some categories have so many free samples available that you could go months without buying certain products.

Here is a category-by-category breakdown of what is out there, which types of free samples are the most generous, and how to get the most out of each one.

Beauty and Skincare

Generosity level: Very high

The beauty and skincare industry is hands-down the most generous when it comes to free samples. There are more free beauty samples available at any given time than any other category, and the quality tends to be excellent. Here is why: beauty brands know that once you try a product you like, you are likely to buy the full-sized version. A $3 sample packet can turn into a $40 moisturizer purchase. The math works in their favor.

What you can expect to find:

  • Skincare: Moisturizers, serums, cleansers, sunscreens, eye creams, face masks. These are the most common free samples in the beauty space. Brands like CeraVe, La Roche-Posay, Neutrogena, and Kiehl's regularly offer samples.
  • Makeup: Foundation samples, lipstick minis, mascara samples, primer packets. These are slightly less common than skincare but still regularly available.
  • Haircare: Shampoo and conditioner sachets, hair treatment samples, styling product minis. These tend to come in foil packets — enough for one or two uses.
  • Fragrance: Perfume and cologne samples are everywhere. Department store brands especially love giving these away. You can build up quite a collection without ever buying a full bottle.
Tip: High-end and luxury beauty brands are actually more generous with samples than drugstore brands. Companies like Estee Lauder, Clinique, Lancome, and Shiseido frequently offer multi-product sample sets because their products are expensive and they need you to try before you buy.

Food and Snacks

Generosity level: High (but fast to claim)

Food samples are extremely popular, which means they tend to run out quickly. The good news is that new food freebies pop up constantly because food companies are always launching new products, flavors, and formulations.

Common types of food freebies:

  • Snack bars and protein bars: This is one of the most active categories. Brands launching new flavors regularly send out full-sized bars.
  • Coffee and tea: Single-serve coffee pods, tea bags, and instant coffee sachets are common. Specialty and organic coffee brands are especially generous.
  • Condiments and sauces: Hot sauce, salad dressing, marinades, and seasoning packets. These are often full-sized bottles because the cost to the brand is low.
  • Health foods: Plant-based protein powders, superfood mixes, meal replacement shakes, and organic snacks. The health food industry is booming, and new brands use samples heavily to build awareness.
  • Baby food and formula: We will cover this more in the baby section, but formula companies are especially generous because brand loyalty in baby food is incredibly strong.
Speed matters here. Popular food samples can be claimed within hours of going live. If you see a food freebie you want, claim it immediately. Coming back to it tomorrow usually means it is already gone.

Pet Products

Generosity level: High

Pet owners, this is your category. The pet industry spends heavily on sampling because pet owners are passionate about finding the right products for their animals and tend to be very brand-loyal once they find something that works.

  • Dog food and treats: By far the most common pet samples. Major brands and smaller specialty brands alike offer sample bags of kibble, canned food, and treats. Some send enough for several meals.
  • Cat food and treats: Similar to dog products but slightly less common. Wet food and treat samples are the most frequent.
  • Pet supplements: Joint supplements, calming treats, dental chews, and probiotics for pets are a growing sample category.
  • Grooming products: Pet shampoo, flea treatments, and dental care products occasionally show up as samples, though less frequently than food.

The pet sampling market has a nice bonus: many pet food companies run ongoing sample programs where you can sign up once and receive regular samples as they launch new products. These are worth looking for because they keep delivering without you needing to find each individual offer.

Baby and Parenting Products

Generosity level: Very high

If you are a new or expecting parent, the amount of free stuff available to you is remarkable. Baby product companies compete fiercely for new parents because the products you choose in the first year — diapers, formula, wipes, skincare — tend to be the ones you stick with for years.

  • Diapers: Nearly every major diaper brand offers free samples. Pampers, Huggies, Luvs, and eco-friendly brands like Honest and Seventh Generation all have sample programs. Some send packs of 4 to 8 diapers — enough to genuinely test the fit and quality.
  • Baby formula: Formula companies are among the most generous samplers in any industry. Enfamil, Similac, and others offer free sample cans, sometimes containing enough formula for several days. They also often include coupons for future purchases.
  • Baby skincare: Lotions, diaper creams, baby wash, and sunscreen samples are common from brands like Aveeno Baby, Cetaphil Baby, Mustela, and Aquaphor.
  • Wipes: Sample packs of baby wipes are readily available, especially from brands launching new formulations (sensitive skin, water-based, etc.).
Registry boxes are a goldmine. When you create a baby registry at major retailers like Amazon, Target, or Walmart, you can receive a free welcome box stuffed with product samples. Amazon's baby registry box alone can contain $35 or more worth of samples. These are some of the highest-value freebies available anywhere.

Health, Vitamins, and Supplements

Generosity level: Medium to high

The supplement industry has exploded in recent years, and that means more free samples than ever. However, this category requires a little more caution than others because the supplement market also has its share of shady operators.

  • Vitamins and multivitamins: Major brands like Nature Made, Centrum, One A Day, and Garden of Life regularly offer sample packs. These usually contain a week's supply — enough to see if you like the form factor (gummies vs. tablets, for example).
  • Protein powder: Single-serving sachets from brands like Orgain, Vega, Garden of Life, and others are common. Great for figuring out which flavor and type (whey, plant-based, collagen) you prefer before committing to a full tub.
  • Probiotics: Gut health brands are spending heavily on sampling right now. Culturelle, Align, and newer brands regularly offer free trial packs.
  • Pain relief: Topical pain relief creams, patches, and balms (like Biofreeze, Salonpas, and Tiger Balm) offer samples fairly often.
  • Sleep aids and wellness: Melatonin gummies, CBD products (in legal states), magnesium supplements, and stress-relief supplements are an active sampling category.
Watch out for "free trial" traps. Some supplement companies offer a "free trial" that is actually a subscription in disguise. They ship you a "free" bottle but require a credit card, then auto-enroll you in a monthly subscription at full price. Legitimate free samples never require credit card information. If they ask for payment details, it is not a free sample.

Household Products

Generosity level: Medium

Household product samples are less glamorous than beauty or food freebies, but they are practical and can save you real money on things you buy regularly anyway.

  • Laundry detergent: Tide, Persil, Seventh Generation, and other detergent brands offer sample pods or small bottles. These are useful because premium detergents can be expensive, and a sample lets you test cleaning power and scent before buying a full-sized container.
  • Cleaning products: All-purpose cleaners, dish soap, and specialty cleaners (like stainless steel cleaner or bathroom spray) show up as samples periodically. Method, Mrs. Meyer's, and Seventh Generation are frequent samplers.
  • Air fresheners and candles: Scented products are natural for sampling because scent preference is so personal. Febreze, Glade, and smaller candle brands offer samples or small-sized products.
  • Paper products: Toilet paper and paper towel samples are rare but do happen, usually from premium brands trying to convince you their product is worth the higher price.
  • Trash bags: This sounds odd, but premium trash bag brands (like Glad's ForceFlex or Hefty's Ultra Strong) occasionally offer sample packs because they need you to experience the difference to justify the price.

Tips for Getting the Most Free Samples

Regardless of category, these strategies will help you claim more free samples successfully:

  1. Use a dedicated email. Just like with sweepstakes, keep your sample requests separate from your personal email. You will get promotional emails from every brand you request samples from.
  2. Act fast. The best free samples run out quickly, often within hours. When you see something you want, fill out the form right away.
  3. Check daily. New samples are posted every day. Making it a quick daily habit — even just five minutes — means you will catch offers before they run out.
  4. Be patient with delivery. Free samples typically take 4 to 8 weeks to arrive. Some take even longer. Do not worry if nothing shows up for a few weeks — they are coming.
  5. Never pay for shipping on a free sample. Legitimate free samples include free shipping. If a site asks you to pay for shipping on a "free" item, it is not really free — skip it.
  6. Sign up for brand loyalty programs. Many brands send free samples exclusively to loyalty program members. Programs from Sephora, Ulta, P&G Good Everyday, and similar are free to join and regularly offer samples as rewards.

The Bottom Line

Free samples are everywhere if you know where to look. Beauty and baby products are the most generous categories by far, followed closely by food and pet products. Health supplements offer solid sampling opportunities but require more caution. Household products round things out with practical, money-saving freebies. The key across all categories is the same: act fast, use a dedicated email, never pay for anything, and check for new offers regularly. Your mailbox will thank you.
